IM Fell DW Pica SC belongs to the family of the FELL TYPES – a collection of types bequeathed in 1686 to the University of Oxford in 1686 by John Fell, who was Bishop of Oxford and Dean of Christ Church. The FELL TYPE family is characterized by an unrefined look, which can be attributed to the letterform being intentionally uneven. It’s useful for books, essays, magazines and advertisements. They come in different variations to maintain the integrity and legibility of the design. IM Fell DW Pica SC is to be printed at 12.5 points, suitable for body text. The rest of the family are:

  • IM Fell Double Pica
  • IM Fell Double Pica SC
  • IM Fell DW Pica
  • IM Fell English
  • IM Fell English SC
  • IM Fell French Canon
  • IM Fell French Canon SC
  • IM Fell Great Primer
  • IM Fell Great Primer SC
